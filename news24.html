6-1-2018 Matthew Murray frustrated
Pittsburgh Penguins goaltender Matthew Murray feels frustrated about his position in the organization. He feels he's good enough to be a franchise player for the team and wonders why the organization still considers him a core player.

A native of Canada, Matthew Murray was originally drafted to the NHL by the Pittsburgh Penguins in the 3rd round of the 2012 entry draft. He has appeared in 102 NHL games during his career, booking 7 career shutouts and allowing 240 goals. The 23 year old goaltender has played 40 games with the Pittsburgh Penguins this season, allowing 104 goals on 954 while booking 2 shutouts.
6-1-2018 Ivan Hlinka praises Kristian Tanus
Pittsburgh Penguins head coach Ivan Hlinka has praised young right wing Kristian Tanus in his latest interviews. Altough he seems very fond of this highly skilled youngster, he didn't want to comment on his team's strategy concerning the upcoming draft.

Kristian Tanus has played 26 games with Färjestad Karlstad this season, collecting 15+11=26 with 4 PIM.
